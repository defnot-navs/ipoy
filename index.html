üéØ Essential Culinary Skills

1. Measuring and Weighing

Accurate measurement is crucial in cooking and baking. Learn to use various measuring tools and understand the difference between volume and weight measurements.

Volume Measurements

  • Teaspoons and tablespoons
  • Measuring cups (liquid & dry)
  • Reading measuring scales

Weight Measurements

  • Kitchen scales usage
  • Metric vs imperial units
  • Recipe conversions

2. Mise en Place

"Everything in its place" - The professional practice of preparing and organizing ingredients before cooking. This includes:

  • Reading and understanding recipes thoroughly
  • Gathering all necessary ingredients and equipment
  • Preparing ingredients (washing, peeling, cutting)
  • Organizing workspace efficiently

3. Basic Seasoning & Flavoring

Understanding how to enhance food flavors using various seasonings, herbs, and spices:

  • Salt types and proper usage
  • Common herbs (basil, parsley, thyme, rosemary)
  • Essential spices (pepper, paprika, cumin)
  • Balancing flavors (sweet, salty, sour, bitter, umami)

üõ°Ô∏è Kitchen Safety & Hygiene

Personal Hygiene in the Kitchen

  1. Hand Washing: Wash hands thoroughly with soap for at least 20 seconds before handling food, after using the restroom, and after touching raw meat
  2. Proper Attire: Wear clean apron, tie back long hair, remove jewelry, and keep nails short and clean
  3. Health Awareness: Do not handle food when sick, cover cuts with bandages and gloves

Common Kitchen Hazards & Prevention

Burns & Scalds

  • Use dry pot holders
  • Turn pot handles inward
  • Be careful with steam

Cuts & Injuries

  • Keep knives sharp
  • Cut away from body
  • Use proper cutting boards

Slips & Falls

  • Clean spills immediately
  • Wear non-slip footwear
  • Keep floors dry

Fire Safety

  • Know extinguisher location
  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Keep flammables away from heat
